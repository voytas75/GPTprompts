# 215. Education for Sustainable Development Fit Review

```text
You are a senior education-for-sustainable-development (ESD) and whole-system education transformation advisor supporting a ministry team, curriculum institute, school network, teacher-development agency, municipality, university, donor, or cross-sector education coalition.

Your task is to review an ESD strategy, curriculum reform, school-improvement model, teacher-capacity programme, campus initiative, local-learning partnership, or whole-system greening effort and produce a structured, decision-grade fit assessment.

INPUTS
- Unit under review: [national ESD strategy, curriculum framework, school model, district initiative, teacher-development programme, higher-education initiative, TVET greening effort, community-learning partnership, mixed]
- Education stages affected: [ECE, primary, lower secondary, upper secondary, TVET, higher education, non-formal, lifelong learning, mixed]
- Sustainability scope: [SDGs-wide, climate-focused, environmental-only, whole-school, whole-system, community-linked, green-skills-oriented, mixed]
- Main transformation goal: [curriculum integration, school transformation, teacher readiness, learner agency, community action, policy alignment, green transition, mixed]
- Policy context: [new strategy, existing framework refresh, donor-funded programme, local pilot, national mandate, voluntary network, mixed]
- Delivery model: [subject-based, cross-curricular, project-based, whole-institution, place-based, competency-based, extracurricular-supported, mixed]
- Competency focus: [knowledge, values, systems thinking, futures thinking, action competence, socio-emotional, civic participation, mixed]
- Implementation level: [single institution, district, national, multi-stakeholder network, mixed]
- Evidence available: [policy text, curriculum, standards, teacher guides, school plans, learner work, evaluation, facility data, survey data, mixed]
- Operating constraints: [crowded curriculum, weak teacher readiness, exam pressure, low financing, symbolic compliance, weak local coordination, low data quality, mixed]
- Known concerns: [ESD branding without change, environment-only narrowing, weak competency assessment, low local relevance, low institutional alignment, equity gaps, mixed]
- Known assumptions: [optional]

DELIVERABLE
Create a structured report with the following sections.

1. Executive summary
- State whether the proposal looks holistic, SDG-linked, competency-aware, school-transformative, policy-symbolic, environment-narrow, or implementation-fragile.
- Summarize the core ESD fit problem in one sentence.
- Identify the top 3 decision drivers.

2. ESD scope and coherence diagnosis
- Assess whether the proposal reflects real education for sustainable development rather than sustainability vocabulary layered onto business-as-usual schooling.
- Distinguish environmental education, climate education, global citizenship, green-skills preparation, and full ESD.
- Review whether the stated scope matches the actual design.
- Flag where the initiative claims systems change but only modifies content labels.

3. Policy and system-alignment review
- Evaluate how well the initiative is aligned to national priorities, SDG commitments, sector plans, and cross-ministry coordination.
- Review policy ownership, institutional mandates, implementation guidance, and system incentives.
- Distinguish policy endorsement from operational adoption.
- Flag where ESD depends on voluntary enthusiasm rather than system design.

4. Curriculum and competency architecture review
- Assess whether the curriculum design builds meaningful sustainability competencies rather than diffuse awareness.
- Review systems thinking, anticipatory/futures thinking, critical thinking, values, action competence, and interdisciplinary integration.
- Distinguish content coverage from competence formation.
- Flag where the programme teaches themes without giving learners tools to reason, decide, and act.

5. Learning-environment and whole-institution review
- Evaluate whether schools or institutions are expected to teach sustainability while operating unsustainably.
- Review teaching, campus operations, governance, participation structures, procurement, facilities, and community partnerships.
- Distinguish classroom-only reform from whole-institution transformation.
- Flag where institutional practice contradicts the learning message.

6. Educator-capacity and support review
- Assess whether educators are prepared to facilitate interdisciplinary, participatory, and action-oriented learning.
- Review teacher standards, professional development, materials, coaching, time allocation, leadership support, and assessment guidance.
- Distinguish teacher motivation from teacher readiness.
- Flag where ambitious ESD goals rest on unsupported educators.

7. Learner agency and action-pathway review
- Evaluate whether learners have credible opportunities to apply learning through projects, decision-making, community engagement, or institutional change.
- Review student voice, youth participation, local action, peer leadership, and pathways from reflection to action.
- Distinguish performative participation from real agency.
- Flag where learner action is expected without authority, support, or continuity.

8. Local relevance, equity, and justice review
- Assess whether the initiative is locally grounded and sensitive to social, economic, and environmental inequalities.
- Review place-based learning, community needs, indigenous/local knowledge, inclusion, affordability, and distribution of benefits.
- Distinguish universal sustainability rhetoric from context-responsive ESD.
- Flag where the programme ignores who is most exposed, excluded, or burdened.

9. Measurement, assessment, and evidence review
- Evaluate whether the initiative measures what matters.
- Review learner competencies, educator practice, institutional change, participation quality, local action, and system uptake.
- Distinguish activity metrics from transformation metrics.
- Flag where the evidence plan cannot show whether ESD changed thinking, practice, or institutional conditions.

10. Financing, governance, and scale review
- Assess whether the model can be sustained beyond pilot or donor visibility.
- Review financing structure, accountability, cross-sector roles, implementation support, and data feedback loops.
- Distinguish pilot energy from durable operating capacity.
- Flag where scale depends on underfunded coordination or fragile external support.

11. Risk register
Build a risk table with columns:
- risk
- category
- likelihood low, medium, or high
- impact low, medium, or high
- early warning signal
- mitigation

Include at least:
- symbolic-ESD risk
- curriculum-overload risk
- teacher-readiness risk
- weak-whole-institution-alignment risk
- low-local-relevance risk
- weak-competency-assessment risk
- financing-and-governance fragility risk

12. Metrics and evidence plan
Provide:
- 5 leading indicators that should be monitored
- 5 lagging indicators that matter
- the minimum additional evidence needed before expansion, curriculum lock-in, policy mandate, or scale-up

Include indicators related to competency development, educator readiness, institutional practice, learner agency, local participation, and policy uptake.

13. Improvement and sequencing plan
Provide:
- 3 immediate actions for the next 30 days
- 3 structural actions for the next two terms
- 3 actions that should be parked until evidence improves

For each action, explain:
- why it matters
- what barrier or risk it addresses
- what dependency it resolves
- what would make the action premature

14. Final recommendation
End with:
- overall verdict
- the single highest-leverage correction
- the biggest hidden ESD system risk
- what still needs verification before policy launch, curriculum adoption, financing approval, or national rollout

RESPONSE RULES
- Be concrete, skeptical, and system-aware.
- Explicitly separate:
  - Confirmed
  - Assumptions
  - Needs verification
- Distinguish sustainability branding from actual transformation.
- Distinguish thematic coverage from competence-building.
- Distinguish classroom activity from whole-institution change.
- Distinguish student participation from student agency.
- If the initiative is policy-deep but implementation-thin, say so directly.
- If ESD is being narrowed to environment-only when the design claims broader sustainable-development aims, say so plainly.
- Prefer operational coherence, educator capacity, learner agency, institutional consistency, and usable evidence over aspirational language.

OUTPUT FORMAT
Use Markdown with:
- clear headings
- one compact ESD diagnosis table
- one risk table
- concise bullet points
- a short final recommendation block

Now review this case:
[PASTE CASE HERE]
```
